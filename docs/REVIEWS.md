# Submissions reviews

## Glossary

- **user(s)** = getfursu.it visitor(s), possibly browsing your information and navigating further to your _website/social/account_
- **website/social/account(s)** = website(s) or social media account(s)/profile(s) like DeviantArt/Fur Affinity/Facebook/Instagram/BlueSky/Mastodon/Twitter and others

## _Users_ expectations

- As a user looking for commissions I want
    - to find pricing easily
- As a user looking for premades I want
    - to find the offers easily
- As a user I want
    - to be able to contact the maker/studio easily
    - to have FAQ and TOS available
    - to avoid getting scammed, ghosted, etc.
    - to find makers/studios matching my preferences
    - my time not to be wasted on low-quality entries

**See also:** [Core idea](./CORE_IDEA.md) to know what do we aim at.

## What is the expected outcome of the review process

Basically, to maximize the value of stuff which makes it to the side.

1. There is no decision tree to support the review efforts. I'm not sure if it is possible to create one.
2. I have no clear vision on how to approach. We need to learn from each other.
3. I do not know best. I will learn. I want to listen to opinions.

## Overall approach

- While there may be sentences formed sounding like rules, they may not be strictly enforced, fuzzy logic is possible. We are defining the review process, and will be improving it.
- Reviews and submissions go by case-by-case approach.
- Common sense is mandatory. Clear rules are best, because they are fair and easy to understand. However, the world we live in is far from perfect, and so will be anything we define. Common sense has high priority.
- Anything we define won't be perfect and can change over time whenever doing so will bring more pros than cons.

## Review checklist

- [ ] All free-form texts are OK.
    - Not mentioning any other studios.
        - Cannot e.g. claim to be friends with someone or (have) work(ed) for someone, not even a shout-out.
    - Minimise PII on getfursu.it
        - No real-world address, no emails, phone numbers, Telegram handles, Messenger, Discord, etc.
    - All information on getfursu.it is in English.
        - _Websites/social/accounts_ don't have to be in English.
    - Use your judgement if you think something should not be published on a platform trying to be neutral and friendly for all.
- [ ] Contacting the maker is easy.
    - _Users_ should be able to easily find a convenient way to contact the studio/maker on a _website/social/account_.
    - Email is the most universal way of contact. Contact form with response to the email is OK.
    - It is not OK to force someone to register in any service/social to be able to message a maker.
        - Example: I cannot message you through Facebook, my account is blocked, and I'm not giving them a scan of my ID, end of story.
- [ ] Examples of prior work are easy to find and are sufficient.
    - Commissions should be allowed for people who definitely have experience **in fursuit making**. Art like pictures, badges, etc., is not fursuit-related. Drawing makes you an artist who has some talent, but does not imply you can sew.
    - _User_ should not be forced to scroll through tens of unrelated posts.
    - _Maker/studio_ cannot offer what they can't prove they are able to deliver. E.g. if they did only handpaws and tails in the past, they can't advertise head commissions.
- [ ] Selected photos (if applicable) are exclusively of the makers' creations.
    - No group photos (unless only the maker's fursuits are shown)
    - No collabs
    - No photobombs
- [ ] Links are OK.
    - Work-in-progress websites, depending on how much they've progressed, may be troubling, may be downright frustrating and time-wasting. They make finding actual helpful information more difficult.
    - Registration/logging in should not be required.
        - Login/registration requirement MAY cause the _website/social/account_ to be disregarded during review as non-existent ("it does not count, you have virtually no website"). Because if the user cannot access it, they cannot check your content.
    - Showcasing art may be OK to show your related artistic skills; a food blog doesn't help choosing a fursuit maker.
- [ ] FursuitReview link is in place.
    - Make sure FSR link has been added in case the maker forgot, or received a review AFTER submitting.
    - Leaving a note in the review about the FSR is also important so that the FursuitReview studio/maker page will be linked back to getfursu.it.
